# Behavioral Science Rationale

Weave is not only a contact finder. It is designed around the emotional and behavioral barriers that make student outreach hard.

For the hackathon prompt, **Build something that solves a real pain point in your personal life**, Weave is intentionally personal: it targets the moment when a student has technical ability but no clear social path into the companies they care about.

## The Pain Point

For students without a strong network, online applications often feel like a silent rejection loop. They submit resumes into applicant tracking systems, wait, hear nothing, and start interpreting silence as evidence that they are not impressive enough.

That silence creates avoidance. The student may know that networking helps, but the next action is ambiguous:

- Who should I message?
- What if I sound needy?
- What if I mention the wrong thing?
- What if they ignore me too?
- When is it appropriate to follow up?

Weave matters because it lowers the emotional cost of taking the next step.

## Behavioral Principles

### Reduce Cognitive Load

"Network at WestJet" is too vague to act on. Weave turns it into a sequence:

1. Research the company.
2. Identify relevant people.
3. Pick who to contact first.
4. Draft a message with proof.
5. Follow up after a reasonable interval.

Clear next actions reduce decision paralysis.

### Specificity Builds Trust

Generic outreach feels spammy because it could be sent to anyone. Weave pushes messages toward concrete details:

- The student's projects and goals.
- The company's current context.
- The contact's role or likely connection to the target team.
- A small, respectful ask.

Specificity makes the message feel honest instead of performative.

### Proof Reduces Desperation

Students often overcompensate in cold messages by sounding either too formal or too eager. Weave centers a healthier structure:

- Hook: why this contact or company.
- Proof: what the student has built or learned.
- Fit: why the conversation makes sense.
- Ask: a low-friction next step.

This lets the student communicate ambition without pleading.

### Implementation Intentions Improve Follow-Through

Follow-ups fail when they live only in memory. Weave turns "I should follow up sometime" into an implementation intention: this person, this stage, this next nudge.

The follow-up pipeline is not just CRM plumbing. It protects momentum during a stressful job search.

### Reduce Shame

The product assumes the student is not lazy or incapable. They are operating in an opaque system with weak feedback. Weave gives them structure, language, and a concrete plan so they can act with confidence.

## Product Behaviors Connected To The Science

| Pain | Weave Behavior |
|------|----------------|
| "Nobody replies to my applications" | Reframes the loop around people and conversations. |
| "I do not know who matters" | Ranks contacts and explains why each person may be relevant. |
| "My message sounds generic" | Uses student profile, company research, and contact context. |
| "I am afraid of sounding desperate" | Encourages proof-backed, low-pressure outreach. |
| "I forget to follow up" | Moves contacts through a visible follow-up pipeline. |
| "This process is overwhelming" | Uses pins, tabs, and selected contacts to keep one company search contained. |

## Why This Fits A Hackathon

The concept is personally grounded, technically interesting, and emotionally legible in a short demo. Judges can understand the pain quickly because most people have experienced silence after applying for something important. Weave makes that silence actionable.
