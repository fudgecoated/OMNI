# Source Notes For Slide Deck

Use these selectively. The main deck should include only two or three research-backed facts, with the rest saved for speaker notes or appendix slides.

## Best Main-Deck Hooks

### 1. Outreach Anxiety

**Slide line:** The anxiety is loud. The upside is invisible.

**Support:** Psychology research suggests people underestimate how much others appreciate being reached out to.

**Use in deck:** Problem or behavioral science slide.

**Citation:** American Psychological Association, "The Surprise of Reaching Out: Appreciated More Than We Think"
https://www.apa.org/pubs/journals/releases/psp-pspi0000402.pdf

### 2. Surface Area Of Luck

**Slide line:** Cold outreach increases the surface area of luck.

**Support:** Every relevant person who knows what you are building, learning, or looking for becomes a possible source of information, introductions, referrals, advice, collaborators, or hidden opportunities.

**Use in deck:** Big idea, close, or transition into Weave's connection loop.

**Citation:** This is a synthesis line based on the weak-ties and outreach framing, not a direct research quote.

### 3. Weak Ties

**Slide line:** Close friends give you support. Weak ties give you reach.

**Support:** Mark Granovetter's "strength of weak ties" theory explains why acquaintances often bring information from outside your close circle.

**Use in deck:** Research slide or speaker note for why Weave focuses on relevant people beyond your immediate network.

**Citation:** Stanford News, "The strength of weak ties"
https://news.stanford.edu/stories/2023/07/strength-weak-ties

### 4. LinkedIn Scale Evidence

**Slide line:** Weak ties can move careers, especially in digital industries.

**Support:** A 2022 Science study involving more than 20 million LinkedIn users found causal evidence that weak ties can increase job mobility.

**Use in deck:** Technical/product credibility slide or appendix.

**Citations:**
- Science, "A causal test of the strength of weak ties"
  https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.abl4476
- MIT News, "The power of weak ties in gaining new employment"
  https://news.mit.edu/2022/weak-ties-linkedin-employment-0915

### 5. Hidden Opportunity Layer

**Slide line:** The best opportunities are often not posted. They are passed.

**Support:** Many useful opportunities travel through informal social channels: introductions, referrals, quiet hiring, events, collaborations, and "you should talk to my friend" moments.

**Use in deck:** Insight slide before the solution.

**Citation:** Use as a narrative synthesis. Pair with weak-ties citations if a source is needed.

## Appendix-Level Research

### Economic Connectedness

**Slide line:** Your network does not just give you opportunities. It changes your sense of what is normal.

**Support:** Raj Chetty and collaborators found economic connectedness, friendships across socioeconomic class, to be among the strongest predictors of upward income mobility identified to date.

**Use in deck:** Appendix or long-form speaker note. It is powerful, but broader than the core demo.

**Citations:**
- Nature, "Social capital I: measurement and associations with economic mobility"
  https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-022-04996-4
- Harvard Gazette, "How childhood friendships sway economic mobility"
  https://news.harvard.edu/gazette/story/2022/08/how-childhood-friendships-sway-economic-mobility/

### Dormant Ties

**Slide line:** An old contact is a warm bridge to a new world.

**Support:** Research on dormant ties suggests reconnecting can create value because old contacts combine prior trust with new information.

**Use in deck:** Future expansion, relationship graph, or appendix.

**Citation:** PubsOnline, "Selecting the Most Valuable (vs. Most Preferred) Dormant Ties"
https://pubsonline.informs.org/doi/10.1287/orsc.2015.0996

### Weak-Tie Wellbeing

**Slide line:** Small interactions can make the world feel more open.

**Support:** Research on weak ties and stranger interactions suggests small social interactions can increase positive experience, belonging, and social connectedness.

**Use in deck:** Optional behavioral appendix, not the main pitch.

**Citations:**
- PubMed, "Mistakenly seeking solitude"
  https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25019381/
- Sage Journals, "Social Interactions and Well-Being"
  https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/abs/10.1177/0146167214529799

## Lines To Use Carefully

| Line | Use |
|------|-----|
| "Cold outreach is the API for accessing the hidden opportunity layer of society." | Strong for a technical audience, but may sound too abstract for the main emotional opener. |
| "A 5% response rate sounds bad until 100 messages create 5 conversations." | Useful if discussing feasibility, but can sound spammy if not framed as thoughtful and relevant outreach. |
| "Your peer group is an invisible curriculum." | Strong but broad. Save for vision or appendix. |
| "Do not only chase status. Chase proximity to the path." | Good for explaining target selection, especially alumni and people 1-3 steps ahead. |

## Recommended Main Deck Research Set

Use this trio:

1. People underestimate how much others appreciate being reached out to.
2. Weak ties bring new information and reach.
3. The best opportunities are often passed through networks, not found in public listings.

This supports Weave without turning the deck into an academic literature review.
