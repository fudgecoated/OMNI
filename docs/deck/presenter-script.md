# Weave Presenter Script

This is a short two-person script for the 5-slide deck. Keep it conversational. The goal is to make judges feel the pain, understand the loop, and believe the team built something real.

## Timing

Target: 2.5-3.5 minutes.

- Presenter 1: slides 1-2.
- Presenter 2: slides 3-4.
- Presenter 1 or 2: slide 5 close.

## Slide 1: Hook

**Presenter 1**

"Most people are not short on effort. They are short on access.

The right conversation can change your trajectory, but most people do not know who to contact, what to say, or how to follow up. That is the pain Weave starts from.

We started with job search because that is where this pain is sharpest for us personally, but the bigger problem is useful connections. Referrals, mentors, collaborators, customers, advisors, and hidden opportunities often start with one awkward message."

## Slide 2: Big Idea

**Presenter 1**

"The best opportunities are often not posted. They are passed.

Research around weak ties shows that acquaintances can unlock information your close circle may never see. But knowing that does not make outreach easy. People worry they will sound annoying, fake, or desperate.

So the question is not just, 'How do we apply to more things?' It is, 'How do we find the right person and start a credible conversation?'"

## Slide 3: Solution

**Presenter 2**

"Weave makes that first human step concrete.

The loop is simple: build a profile, find relevant people, understand why each person matters, draft a credible message, and follow up.

The important part is that Weave is not just writing generic messages. It combines the candidate's proof, the company context, and the contact's relevance so the outreach feels specific and low-pressure."

## Slide 4: Product Proof

**Presenter 2**

"This slide shows the core loop.

First, Weave finds a relevant person and explains why they are worth contacting. Then it helps draft a message grounded in real context. Finally, it keeps the follow-up visible, because a useful connection is a loop, not one message.

That is what makes Weave different from a job board or a generic AI writer. Every contact becomes a reason, a message, and a next step."

## Slide 5: Technical Quality And Vision

**Presenter 1 or 2**

"Technically, Weave is a full-stack TypeScript app with a React/Vite frontend, an Express backend, shared TypeScript contracts, AI-assisted research, finder reasoning, chat coaching, and skill-based prompts.

We also built cached and seeded demo paths so the project is reliable for judging. The MVP is honest: demo login, lightweight persistence, no automated LinkedIn sending, and a complete judged loop.

Our vision is bigger than job search. Weave helps people make useful connections less accidental."

## Key Points To Keep In Mind

- Lead with access, not jobs: jobs are the wedge, useful connections are the larger vision.
- Repeat the loop: find the right person, understand why, draft the message, follow up.
- Do not call it just an "AI job search tool." Call it a connection workspace.
- Emphasize that AI is core to research, reasoning, coaching, and drafting.
- Be honest about MVP scope: demo auth, cached/seeded data, local/JSON persistence, no automated LinkedIn sending.
- Say the pain clearly: people know effort matters, but effort without access often disappears into silence.
- Make Slide 4 the proof moment. It should show that the product is complete enough to use.
- Close with: "Make useful connections less accidental."

## If Judges Ask What Is New

"The creative part is the connection loop. We are not optimizing resumes or scraping job posts. We are helping someone decide who to contact, why that person matters, what to say, and how to keep the relationship moving."

## If Judges Ask About Feasibility

"We scoped the MVP honestly. It uses local/demo auth and lightweight persistence, but the core flow is complete. The cached WestJet and seed paths make the demo reliable, while the AI-backed path supports live company research and coaching."
