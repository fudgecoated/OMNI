# Outreach Messaging

Use **all** context on the pin: company research, job role, and the applicant narrative (`narrativeForAI`).

## Before drafting

Confirm you have:

- Company brief (product team, news, stack)
- Role level and talking points
- Applicant: who they are, why they're searching, projects, can/cannot claim, learning

## Subject line (cold email)

- **Max ~50–60 characters** — must read cleanly in inbox previews
- Prefer: `UCalgary SWE intern — intro` or `Quick intro — SWE intern @ Google`
- **Do not** pack school + full role + full team + company into one subject
- **Avoid** arrows (`→`), long team names, or product codenames in the subject
- Use short school labels (`UCalgary`, not `University of Calgary`)

## Message structure (cold email / DM)

Use **3–5 short paragraphs** separated by a blank line. Never one wall of text.

1. **Greeting + identity** — one sentence (name, year, school, location)
2. **Hook** — one or two sentences tying company/team/news to why you're reaching out
3. **Proof** — one project with outcome (from `projects`); optional one short “on the side” line only if brief
4. **Ask** — 15 minutes, no pressure
5. **Sign-off** — thanks + name

Optional (pick **at most one**): learning focus **or** stack alignment — not both unless very short.

## Connection request (short)

- First name only
- One line identity + one proof point
- Optional school/team tie
- "Would love to connect"
- Single paragraph; under ~300 characters

## Personalization checklist

- [ ] Mentions a real product/team from company research (short team label in subject if needed)
- [ ] Uses exactly one project from applicant profile
- [ ] Reflects `jobSearchWhy` without sounding desperate
- [ ] Does **not** claim anything in `skillsAvoid`
- [ ] Does **not** say message was sent by AI or Hermes
- [ ] Under ~150 words for first DM unless user asks for longer
- [ ] Paragraph breaks between logical blocks

## Punctuation

- **Do not use en dashes (–), em dashes (—), or spaced hyphens as separators** in subjects or body copy.
- Use commas, periods, or colons instead (e.g. "Quick intro, SWE intern @ Google" not "Quick intro — SWE intern").
- Hyphens inside compound words (e.g. "3rd-year") are fine.

## Tone

Warm, professional, peer-to-peer. No "I am passionate about dynamic synergies."
